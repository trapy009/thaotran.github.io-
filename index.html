My Journey in Technology

Since I was exposed to MYOB DevelopHer program last year, I have become more curious about the Tech industry and Software Development in specific. After my first unsuccessful attempt to get into the program, I have reflected on the journey and have decided not to let setbacks set me back.

What is Software Development? How do I become a Software Developer? More and more questions about being a Software Developer have come to my mind. I started to research about required technological skills, read stories of women in Technology, listen to Ted Talks about coding. The more I research the more curious I am. “I need to do this! I can do it!”, this voice in my head constantly motivates me to move forward. I came across a LinkedIn Learning path called “Become a Software Developer” and have been working through this path. It has provided me knowledge and a broad perspective on core technologies for web development, software development and databases, like HTML, CSS, JavaScript…

And here I am, showing you my work that I have self learnt so far.

The reasons that keep me going and trying

Why Do I Reapply for DevelopHer Program?

I have always been taught to avoid risk and failure since I was young. My parents taught me stability is important. I grow up and work in jobs that I am told I am good at. I keep working for security until I came across DevelopHer and found my excitement to start something new, challenging and changing the world.

Technology has a huge impact on inventing and disrupting conventions to keep us continually growing. I want to be a part of it. I want to learn and work in the Tech industry where I could go after the unconventional ideas to build something better for our society. Businesses play a very important role in our life and MYOB’s mission of helping businesses in Australia and New Zealand to start, survive and succeed has made me thrive to be a part of the team.

I reapply for DevelopHer as I have learnt to accept my imperfections and failure. I would like to get back up where I fell and be brave to keep chasing the goal that excites and challenges me.
